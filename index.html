Why Recycling is must?

Recycling is an important practice that helps conserve natural resources, reduce waste in landfills, save energy, reduce greenhouse gas emissions, create jobs, and support the economy. By recycling, we can reduce our impact on the environment and help build a more sustainable and resilient future. It's important for individuals, businesses, and governments to make recycling a part of their daily practices to ensure that we are doing our part to protect the planet and its resources.

How work is done?

At Remine, we have our own fleet of trucks and containers as well as tie-ups with number of leading logistics companies to support our initiative of returning back the basic commodities to the respective industries without further withdrawal from mines. This robust network of reverse logistic will certainly promote formal recycling of e-waste. We hope that the above mechanism will certainly prevent informal and unscientific disposal and its subsequent hazards to human health and the environment. As an ongoing activity, Remine will continue to establish more & more collection points to map the whole nation in a very structured manner. Remine will provide three-tier architecture to OEMs to meet their EPR obligations, last mile collection points, state level dismantling centres and national level recycling centres. Remine endeavour to be the first Indian companies to envisage an integrated reverse logistics network to facilitate effective collection of e-waste from end consumers across the country.


LI Batteries Recycling Work Flow

The waste lithium-ion battery recycling and processing equipment of our company will crush the materials step by step through shredders, crushers and grinders, and separate them by air separation facilities. The whole set of equipment adopts a closed negative pressure system to reduce dust pollution, and has a pulse dust collection facilities collect dust generated during the separation process and subsequent processes.
